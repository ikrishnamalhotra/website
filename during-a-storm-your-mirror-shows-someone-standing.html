# Reflected Terror

Introduction:

The wind howled like a banshee, rattling the windows of my old Victorian house. Rain lashed against the glass, blurring the world outside into a swirling, grey chaos. I was alone, huddled in my room, trying to lose myself in a book, but the storm's fury kept tugging at my nerves. Lightning flashed, momentarily illuminating the room, and in that brief, stark light, I glanced at the antique mirror hanging on my wall. It was an ornate thing, a family heirloom, with a heavy, gilded frame that always seemed a little too imposing. Usually, I paid it no mind. But tonight, something was terribly, irrevocably wrong.

The Reflection:

Another lightning strike. Another flash. This time, my heart leaped into my throat. In the mirror, I saw myself, pale and wide-eyed. But behind my reflection, standing just over my shoulder, was another figure. Tall and gaunt, wreathed in shadow. Its face was obscured, hidden in darkness, but I could sense its presence, a cold, malevolent energy that seemed to seep out of the glass and into my soul.

I whipped around, my chair scraping against the wooden floor. Nothing. Just the empty space behind me, the storm raging outside.

It was just a trick of the light, I told myself, my voice trembling. Just my imagination running wild.

I tried to focus on my book, but the image was burned into my mind. The shadowy figure, impossibly close. I couldn't shake the feeling that I was being watched.

The Game of Glimpses:

I couldn't resist. I glanced back at the mirror. This time, the figure was clearer. Though the face was still hidden in shadow, I could make out the distinct shape of a long, thin hand resting on my shoulder. Not my shoulder, but the shoulder of my reflection.

A wave of nausea washed over me. I forced myself to look away, to breathe. I told myself I was exhausted, stressed from school, letting the storm get to me. But deep down, I knew this was something more, something far more sinister.

The next flash of lightning brought a fresh wave of terror. The figure was closer now. I could see the faint outline of eyes glowing in the darkness. They were fixed on me, cold and predatory. And then, a chilling detail: a thin, almost invisible thread of smoke seemed to curl from the figure's mouth.

Each flash brought the figure closer, clearer. It was as if it were slowly pulling itself out of the mirror, into my reality.

Confrontation:

The storm reached its peak. The house groaned under the relentless assault of wind and rain. I stared into the mirror, paralyzed with fear. The figure was practically standing on top of me now. I could feel its breath on my neck, cold and stale like the air in a tomb.

Finally, the shadowy face began to resolve, revealing sunken eyes, a gaunt, skeletal face and a thin, cruel mouth pulled back in a perpetual snarl. The smoke emanated from this maw like a whisper of death itself.

"Who are you?" I managed to choke out, my voice barely audible above the storm.

The figure didn't speak, but the reflection in the mirror twisted into a grotesque mockery of my own face. The eyes, the mouth, the very skin seemed to melt and reform into something utterly alien and horrifying.

Then, the reflection reached out, its hand passing through the glass. I felt an icy grip on my shoulder, digging into my flesh.

Escape?

I screamed, a primal sound of pure terror. I ripped myself away from the mirror, stumbled backwards, and slammed into my desk. The impact sent books and papers flying.

I scrambled to my feet, my heart pounding in my chest, and ran. I didn't know where I was going, I just had to get away from the mirror, from the figure that was now undoubtedly free from its glassy prison.

I burst out of my room and down the stairs, the house creaking and groaning around me. I didn't dare look back.

I reached the front door, fumbled with the lock, and threw it open. The wind and rain lashed at me, but I didn't care. I plunged out into the storm, desperate to escape the horror that was now lurking inside my house.

Conclusion:

I ran until I couldn't run anymore, until my lungs burned and my legs trembled. I collapsed on the rain-soaked ground, gasping for breath, the storm raging around me. Safe? I don't know what will come next.

Now, days later, every reflective surface sends shivers down my spine. Every shadow seems to hold a hidden threat. Because I know, with a certainty that chills me to the bone, that it’s not just the mirror I need to fear. It's the fact that whatever that thing is, it's not trapped anymore. And it's still looking for me. And maybe...it's standing right behind me now.
